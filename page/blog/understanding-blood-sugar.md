# Understanding Blood Sugar Levels

Blood sugar (glucose) is your body's main source of energy. Keeping it within a healthy range is crucial for everyone, but especially for people with diabetes.

## What Are Normal Blood Sugar Levels?

| Time | Normal Range |
|------|-------------|
| Fasting | 70–99 mg/dL (3.9–5.5 mmol/L) |
| 2 hours after eating | Less than 140 mg/dL (7.8 mmol/L) |
| Before bed | 100–140 mg/dL (5.6–7.8 mmol/L) |

## Tips to Maintain Healthy Blood Sugar

### Diet
- Choose foods with a low glycemic index
- Eat regular, balanced meals
- Limit sugary drinks and processed foods
- Include plenty of fiber-rich vegetables

### Exercise
- Aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate activity daily
- Strength training can improve insulin sensitivity
- Check your blood sugar before and after exercise

### Monitoring
Regular monitoring helps you understand how different foods and activities affect your blood sugar. Use a continuous glucose monitor (CGM) or traditional glucometer to track your levels.

## When to See a Doctor

Contact your healthcare provider if you experience:
- Frequent episodes of high or low blood sugar
- Symptoms of hyperglycemia (extreme thirst, frequent urination)
- Symptoms of hypoglycemia (shakiness, confusion, sweating)
